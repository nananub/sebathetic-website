SEB
ATHETIC
Poem by Nana Nguyen

Soft static hum behind the wall,
Light from a screen where shadows fall.

The ashtray full, the coffee cold,
His thoughts too loud, his spine too old.

Curtains never drawn since June,
He sleeps beneath a broken moon.

The frogs are singing far away,
He counts regrets to end the day.

The world is calling through the screen,
A place he’s been but never seen.

The code blinks like a dying star,
He’s home, and yet, he’s still too far.

The keys click soft like falling rain,
The silence amplifies the pain.

No doors are locked, but none are wide,
He’s not alone, just stuck inside.
